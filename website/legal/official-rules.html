Game Modes & Prize Structure

Flat-Rate Escalator

Round N costs $0.01 × N (Round 1 = $0.01 … Round 100 = $1.00). Maximum possible spend per player: $50.50. Prize pool is 60% of aggregate entry fees; 40% is retained as a platform fee. Paid places scale with the field: under 15 players pays the top 3 (50% / 30% / 20%), 15–39 the top 5, and 40+ roughly the top 10%. The schedule is shown before entry.

Streak Saver

Same per-round pricing, but a correct answer makes the next round free; only an incorrect answer charges the round fee.

Milestone Booster ("Treasure Hunt")

Same per-round pricing as the Flat-Rate Escalator (Round N costs $0.01 × N). Every 10th round from Round 10 through Round 90 is a "clue": a correct answer earns a clue worth an escalating multiplier of that round's own cost (multiplier = round ÷ 10, so Round 20 = 2× its 20¢ cost = 40¢, Round 90 = 9× its 90¢ cost = $8.10) — nothing is credited to the player's balance yet. Round 100 is the "final answer": a correct answer credits every clue collected during the game to the player's balance in one lump sum, as non-withdrawable bonus tokens (up to $28.50 if all 9 clues were collected); an incorrect answer on Round 100 forfeits the entire collected total — no payout, regardless of how many clues were earned. Missing a clue round (10–90) simply forfeits that clue with no other penalty. The prize pool is funded solely by player entry fees (60% pool / 40% platform fee), the same as the other modes; the Treasure Hunt payout never adds to or draws from it.

Sudden Death Overtime

Applies to all three modes: if two or more players are tied for a top-3 finish at Round 100, a live, shrinking-timer tiebreaker resolves it at a flat entry fee per question.

Streak Bonus ("3 the Hard Way")

Applies to all three modes: after three consecutive correct answers, and for every correct answer while that streak continues, the round's cost is credited back to the player's balance as non-withdrawable bonus tokens. This affects how far a player's funds carry them in a single tournament only — it does not add to, or draw from, the prize pool, and has no effect on withdrawable cash or payouts.
