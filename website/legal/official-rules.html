Game Modes & Prize Structure

Flat-Rate Escalator

Round N costs $0.01 × N (Round 1 = $0.01 … Round 100 = $1.00). Maximum possible spend per player: $50.50. Prize pool is 60% of aggregate entry fees; 40% is retained as a platform fee. How that pool is split across finishers is one of the Prize Distribution schemes below.

Streak Saver

Same per-round pricing, but a correct answer makes the next round free; only an incorrect answer charges the round fee.

Milestone Booster

Flat per-tier pricing (Bronze 10¢ / Silver 25¢ / Gold 50¢ / Platinum $1.00). The prize pool is funded solely by player entry fees (60% pool / 40% platform fee), the same as the other modes.

Prize Distribution (applies to all three modes)

The prize pool is split across finishers according to one of four schemes. Staff assign one scheme to each game at creation — it is not tied to which Game Mode is being played — and it is disclosed, along with the resulting number of paid places, before you enter that specific game.

  • Standard (the default): scales with field size — fewer than 15 eligible players pays the top 3 (50% / 30% / 20%), 15–39 pays the top 5, and 40+ pays roughly the top 10% (a decaying podium plus a flat minimum-cash tail).
  • Classic Top 3: always pays only the top 3 finishers, 50% / 30% / 20%, regardless of field size.
  • Winner-Take-Most: pays only the top 3 finishers, weighted 70% / 20% / 10%.
  • Spread the Wealth: pays roughly the top 25% of the field (minimum 5 places) on a gentle decay.

Sudden Death Overtime

Applies to all three modes: if two or more players are tied for a top-3 finish at Round 100, a live, shrinking-timer tiebreaker resolves it at a flat entry fee per question.

Streak Bonus ("3 the Hard Way")

Applies to all three modes: after three consecutive correct answers, and for every correct answer while that streak continues, the round's cost is credited back to the player's balance as non-withdrawable bonus tokens. This affects how far a player's funds carry them in a single tournament only — it does not add to, or draw from, the prize pool, and has no effect on withdrawable cash or payouts.
